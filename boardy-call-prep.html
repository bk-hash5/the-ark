PAGE 2: YOUR GAME PLAN FOR THE CALL

Your Positioning

You’re a founder building an AI-powered Bitcoin Lightning payment platform (The Ark / thenode.it.com). You want to: 1. Get into their network as a founder 2. Get matched with seed-stage investors interested in AI + Bitcoin/Lightning 3. Eventually raise a $1M seed round

What to Tell Boardy About You

  • What you’re building: AI agent platform that accepts Bitcoin Lightning payments — 120+ AI services, pay-per-task, no subscriptions
  • Why it’s unique: Intersection of AI + Bitcoin Lightning (L402 protocol) — micropayments for AI services, no credit cards needed
  • Market: Global, especially emerging markets / unbanked populations who can’t use traditional payment rails
  • Traction: Live product at thenode.it.com, real users, listed on Satring directory
  • What you need: $1M seed to scale — marketing, infrastructure, team

Questions to Ask Them

  1. “How many investors in your network focus on AI + fintech/crypto?”
  2. “What stage investors do you match founders with most? Pre-seed? Seed?”
  3. “What’s the typical timeline from onboarding to first intro?”
  4. “How many intros does the average founder get?”
  5. “Is there a cost for founders, or is it free?” (It’s free for founders — confirm this)
  6. “Can you share any success stories of founders who raised through Boardy intros?”
  7. “How does the $200M fund work — can Boardy’s own fund invest in companies it matches?”

Investor Types You’d Be Matched With

Based on their network, expect intros to: - Pre-seed/Seed VCs ($250K–$2M checks) — Precursor, Afore, NextView types - Crypto/Bitcoin-focused VCs — if they have them in network - AI-focused funds — hot space right now - Angels — individual investors writing $25K–$100K checks - Accelerators — Y Combinator, Techstars types

What $1M Seed Typically Looks Like

  • Usually a lead investor puts in 30-50% ($300K-$500K)
  • Rest filled by 3-8 smaller investors/angels
  • You’ll need: pitch deck, clear metrics, team story
  • Typical seed valuation for AI startups: $5M-$15M pre-money
  • You’d give up roughly 10-20% equity

Do’s and Don’ts

DO: - ✅ Be specific about what you’re building and why YOU are the person to build it - ✅ Mention the Bitcoin Lightning + AI intersection — it’s a niche nobody owns yet - ✅ Talk about the Africa/emerging markets angle — VCs love underserved market plays - ✅ Show you have a live product (not just an idea) - ✅ Ask about their success metrics — shows you’re serious

DON’T: - ❌ Don’t oversell or exaggerate traction - ❌ Don’t say you “need” the money (say you want to “accelerate growth”) - ❌ Don’t commit to anything on the call — this is exploratory - ❌ Don’t ask for money directly — Boardy connects, they don’t invest (mostly) - ❌ Don’t be vague — “I’m building an AI thing” is death. Be specific.

Your One-Liner (Practice This)

“I’m building The Ark — an AI platform where anyone in the world can access 120+ AI services and pay with Bitcoin Lightning. No credit cards, no subscriptions, just micropayments. We’re live, we’re growing, and we’re looking for seed investors who understand AI and emerging markets.”

Bottom line: Boardy is a door-opener, not a deal-closer. Your job on this call is to get into their system with a strong profile so they match you with the right investors. Be clear, be specific, be memorable.
